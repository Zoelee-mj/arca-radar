// SUMMARY

The two products and where they belong

Brevis is moving beyond ZK infrastructure into two AI-native products. They enter the market from different angles but sell to the same core audience — AI developers and builders — so the recommendation is a single event engine with two messaging tracks, concentrated where both products convert: the AI agent and coding hackathon circuit and the AI-engineer builder community.

Two products, one core audience

Mapping both products' users shows a hard overlap. The intersection — AI agent & coding hackathons plus the AI-engineer builder community — is where one event reaches both buyers at once. Auto-Research leans to the deep edge (systems, infra); ARCA leans to the surface (devtools, data); the centre is shared.

// ROADMAP

Twelve-month plan

Each phase is tied to named platforms and a measurable KPI.

Phase 1 · 0–3 months

Learn & map

Understand the ecosystem, build the network, track communities — at near-zero cost.
  • Events Radar live across Luma + Devpost + lablab (done)
  • Attend S-tier meetups on Luma
  • Join 2–3 hackathons as participants, not sponsors
KPI · communities mapped + contacts made + radar shipped
Phase 2 · 3–6 months

Sponsor & co-host

Convert visibility into a first developer cohort.
  • Sponsor a Devpost / lablab challenge track
  • Co-host a Luma meetup
  • Demo Auto-Research at AI-engineering events
KPI · builders engaged + sign-ups + submissions
Phase 3 · 6–12 months

Own the stage

Position Brevis as a recognized AI-infrastructure brand.
  • Host the Brevis AI Research Meetup
  • Launch the Auto-Research Hackathon
  • Secure speaking slots at flagship conferences
KPI · attendees + inbound partnerships + brand mentions
